Executive Takeaway

ALFA International is an 88-year-old, third-generation family firm with genuine operational excellence — 95% same-day shipping, 5,000+ dealer and service-tech relationships, and a deep catalog serving Hobart, Bizerba, Globe, and Berkel equipment across thousands of commercial kitchens. The digital presence, however, does not yet reflect any of that. The website is catalog-first and the blog has been silent since April 2020. The Instagram and YouTube channels exist but are effectively dormant. The Facebook link in the site footer points to a page that no longer loads. Brad Lundquist has a real and distinctive leadership voice on LinkedIn (CEN Conference with Marshall Goldsmith, Saint Sebastian keynote in Switzerland, Bocconi recruiting), but that voice isn't yet shaping company narrative. The opportunity is significant and specific: the business has the substance — the heritage, the relationships, the discipline — that most competitors are trying to manufacture. It simply isn't visible in the channels where the next generation of buyers, service techs, and dealers now look first.

How to Build Legendary Brand Visibility

Short Term (0–6 months)

Activate Brad's LinkedIn Voice

Establish a consistent LinkedIn cadence from Brad on the foodservice category, the family-business perspective, what ALFA sees across thousands of commercial kitchens, and the relationships that have defined 88 years. Re-awaken the Website Story
The blog has been dormant since April 2020. Relaunch it — not as a product catalog, but as a point of view. Stories from the ALFA warehouse, from the three generations of family leadership, from service techs who have relied on ALFA for decades. Evolve the site from 'what ALFA sells' to 'why ALFA matters.' Declare the heritage, the craft, and the relationships on the homepage, not buried on the About page.
Launch a Service-Tech Content Engine
Service technicians are one of ALFA's three primary audiences and the group most underserved by the current footprint. The existing YouTube channel has been dormant for 12 years — a rare second-chance opportunity to activate an already-created property. Start with short diagnostic videos, OEM-equivalent comparison content, and tech tips for the exact equipment ALFA supplies (Hobart mixers, Bizerba slicers, Globe grinders). This is the ground Parts Town has partially colonized through technology; ALFA can out-relationship them with expertise.

Medium Term (6–18 months)

Brand Identity & Visual Story
Develop a distinctive visual identity that communicates craft, heritage, and family-business integrity — not generic B2B parts-distributor aesthetics. Photography of the real people, the real warehouse, the real products. A design language that makes ALFA unmistakable in a sea of lookalike parts sites. The brand should feel like what it is: 88 years of family pride.
Industry Thought Leadership Platform
Launch a newsletter, podcast guest strategy, or byline program to position Brad and ALFA as the voices of the independent, heritage side of the foodservice equipment industry. As Parts Town consolidates (3Wire, IPC, NDCP) and Sysco acquires Edward Don, ALFA's independent family-firm story becomes more valuable, not less — but only if it is told.
Video & YouTube Revival
The existing YouTube channel hasn't been touched in 12 years. That makes activation a matter of strategy, not infrastructure. Build a content calendar around product demos, service-tech how-to content, kitchen stories from customers, and Brad's perspective on the industry. Video is the medium of trust and expertise — and the medium most conspicuously absent from ALFA's actual output. A dozen thoughtful videos in the first six months would reset the footprint meaningfully.
Competitive Landscape

Who's in the Conversation

Parts Town
Digital Category Definer
The $2B ecommerce juggernaut reshaping the entire foodservice parts category. Generates ~70% of sales online, operates Serial Number Lookup, PartSPIN 360° imaging, a mobile app, and shipping until 9 PM ET. Has absorbed 3Wire, IPC, and NDCP. Not a direct comp — a structural threat. Sets the digital expectation every buyer now arrives with.
Hobart Parts (ITW)
OEM Incumbent
The original manufacturer. 40,000+ SKUs, $100M parts inventory, 70+ branches nationwide, 1,500 service trucks, same-day shipping. Defines 'genuine OEM.' ALFA's positioning depends on how convincingly OEM-equivalent can be made to feel like the smarter, relationship-led choice.
Heritage Parts / Wasserstrom / PartsFPS
Multi-Brand Distributors
Mid-market parts distributors with varying degrees of digital sophistication. Like ALFA, they serve dealers and service techs, but most compete primarily on price and catalog breadth. None has yet claimed 'heritage family firm' as a brand moat — the positioning is available.
Sysco / Edward Don
Consolidation Wave
Sysco's acquisition of Edward Don (a major foodservice equipment and supplies dealer) signals accelerating consolidation in the adjacent channel. ALFA's LinkedIn noted the move. As the industry consolidates, the independent, family-owned story becomes more valuable — if it is told loudly enough to be heard.
The Real Insight
Competitive Advantage

The challenge isn't competing on technology with Parts Town or price with Amazon. The challenge is making ALFA's heritage, relationships, and craft impossible to miss in the channels where buyers and service techs now look first.

OEM + Amazon Squeeze
